Most UI libraries hand you the tools and leave the taste up to you. EvilButtons is different — every component ships already beautiful, already animated, already ready to impress. Built with <Link href="https://motion.dev" _blank={true}>Motion</Link> and <Link href="https://shadcn.com/ui" _blank={true}>Shadcn</Link>.

## The Idea

Built by <Link href="https://x.com/radiumcoders" _blank={true}>Jay</Link>, a design engineer who kept asking the same question: *why do so many interfaces feel forgettable?*

Not because beautiful components don't exist — but because building them takes real time. Most teams reach for a library, grab the default, ship it, and move on. EvilButtons exists for the moments when you want something that actually *stops people*.

Every button, every logo, every interaction is handcrafted. Open source. Open code. Yours to use, fork, or remix.

## Why "Evil"?

Not malicious. Just unapologetic.

- Opinionated design that doesn't compromise on aesthetics
- Animations that are deliberate, not decorative
- Components built with SVGs and <Link href="https://motion.dev" _blank={true}>Motion</Link> to feel alive

## Built on Motion

Motion won out over every other animation library for one reason: it gets out of your way. Expressive enough to do anything, simple enough to drop into any project — it's the foundation that makes every EvilButton feel the way it does.
